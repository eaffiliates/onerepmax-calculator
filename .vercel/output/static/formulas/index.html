1RM Formula Explanations

Our calculator uses four proven scientific formulas to estimate your one-rep max. Each has different strengths and optimal rep ranges for maximum accuracy.

Epley Formula

1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps/30)

Best for:

  • Moderate rep ranges (3-8 reps)
  • General strength training
  • Most common and widely used

Accuracy:

Very reliable for 3-8 reps. Tends to slightly overestimate at very low reps (1-2) and underestimate at higher reps (9-12).

Brzycki Formula

1RM = Weight × 36 / (37 - Reps)

Best for:

  • Lower rep ranges (1-6 reps)
  • Powerlifting movements
  • Maximum strength testing

Accuracy:

Most accurate for 1-6 reps. Becomes less reliable above 8 reps. Preferred by powerlifters for its accuracy at low rep ranges.

Lombardi Formula

1RM = Weight × Reps^0.10

Best for:

  • Single rep attempts
  • Elite level athletes
  • Validation of other formulas

Accuracy:

Most conservative estimate. Often underestimates true 1RM, especially for higher rep ranges. Good as a safety baseline.

O'Conner Formula

1RM = Weight × (1 + 0.025 × Reps)

Best for:

  • Higher rep ranges (6-12 reps)
  • Bodybuilding movements
  • Endurance-strength training

Accuracy:

More conservative than Epley. Works well for moderate to higher rep ranges. Often preferred for safety when working with novice lifters.

Quick Comparison

Brzycki 1-6 reps Higher estimate Powerlifting
Lombardi 1-3 reps Conservative Safety baseline
O'Conner 6-12 reps Conservative Higher rep training

Important Considerations

  • • Rep Range Matters: All formulas become less accurate beyond 12 reps. For best results, test between 1-10 reps.
  • • Individual Variation: Your actual 1RM may differ based on training background, muscle fiber type, and lifting technique.
  • • Exercise Specificity: Formulas work best for compound movements (squat, bench, deadlift) and may be less accurate for isolation exercises.
  • • Safety First: These are estimates. Always warm up properly and consider having a spotter for maximum attempts.
